    Frequently asked questions in "Learning El Salvador"

    This part of our blog is to answer a few questions that we are often asked by our Learning El Salvador to us Learning El Salvador. To begin with, one of the most common questions we are asked is about our class schedules.

    Responding to the question "What are our class schedules?" Learning El Salvador's beneficiaries of Learning El Salvador, receive classes on Saturday mornings at the facilities of the the facilities of the National Institute in Colonia Ciudad Obrera, Apopa.
    The schedule is divided as follows:

    ✓ English class for first and second year Project - 7:30 a.m. - 9:30 a.m.
    ✓ Break - 9:30 - 9:45.
    ✓ Values and vocational orientation class for first and second year Project -. 9:45 a.m. - 11:00 a.m.


    How can I apply?

    To be a student of Learning El Salvador, you must be a high school student of the Instituto Nacional de la Colonia Ciudad Obrera de Apopa. Also, you must have a high commitment to remain in the Project.

    Are the classes paid?

    To date, Learning El Salvador does not charge for its classes as a non-profit organization. for its classes because it is a non-profit organization. Classes are taught by volunteers each year.

    How long are classes?

    The project contemplates two years of study on Saturday mornings at INCOA's facilities in Apopa and therefore, a class year has 24 classes made up of three modules.

    Am I eligible?

    If you are a student of INCOA, being this the only institute where we operate so far, you can receive classes once you complete the registration process that starts at the beginning of the year.

    What are the benefits of studying at Learning El Salvador?

    At the end of your classes at Learning El Salvador, you will obtain a basic level of the English language, receive comprehensive training in values and activities that stimulate your skills and vocational you skills and vocational orientation once you finish your classes.
