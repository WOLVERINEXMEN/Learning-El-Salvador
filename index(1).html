    Who are we?

    Learning El Salvador is a Social Project that began operations in April 2018 oriented in the teaching of the English language, practice of values and vocational guidance to young high school graduates of the National Institute of Colonia Ciudad Obrera de Apopa. Currently and since its inception, the base of volunteers in Learning are graduates of different classes of Supérate Raíces.

    Learning El Salvador is one of the winners organizations of the Solidarity Award in the Center ¡Supérate! Fundación Raíces, which is given to organizations that create a project and are based on teaching and helping others.

    What our graduates say

    Testimonies

    Some young graduates tell us how the organization changed their lives.

    "Thanks to this opportunity at Learning El Salvador I have the English skills that have allowed me to acquire my work and respond in a good way in the professional field, so I can continue advancing and also show the values that the teachers instilled in me in each of the class sessions."
    Ashley Torres Contreras
    Graduate of the first class.

    "For me, being part of Project Learning El Salvador was a great experience, because it helped me to acquire new knowledge in English and Values. I thank from the bottom of my heart each volunteer that make up the project, because in addition to teaching both subjects, they gave us a lot of motivational support."
    Adriana Orellana
    Graduate of the fourth class.

    "For me, being part of the Learning El Salvador Project was a great experience. I feel quite happy and satisfied with everything I learned during the English and Values classes, which I received in 2020 and 2021. Currently, I am studying at the University of El Salvador, taking cycle 1-2022 in the profession of Bachelor of Social Work."
    Adriana Isabel
    Graduate of the fourth class.

    "Personally, been in Learning El Salvador has been an incredible experience, the knowledge I acquired I continue to put into practice even after graduating such as having good habits, not being afraid to speak English, making good use of my finances thanks to the financial education topics I received in the area of values. This knowledge and many more are being a great tool in my life today."
    Adriana Calderón
    Graduate of the fourth class.
